About the MiCAR Tracker

What this tracker is

The MiCAR Tracker is a lightweight open project that gathers and digests public information about crypto asset service providers. Its goal is to give the community and industry stakeholders more transparency into market behaviors and compliance-related metrics. We present the data in an easy-to-scan scorecard style so readers can compare providers at a glance.

What data we pull

We aggregate publicly available information including (but not limited to):

  • Publicly posted fees and cost components
  • Compliance flags and regulatory disclosures
  • Service availability and supported assets
  • Public statements and policy documentation

Note: data is sourced from public websites, filings, and provider disclosures. We do not pull private or user-specific data.

Why we built it

Our aim is to digest raw public data into concise, comparable summaries so people — both in the community and the industry — can better understand market dynamics, costs, and compliance posture. Think of the tracker like a simple compass: not a replacement for an audit, but a navigation tool to find areas worth looking into.

About the Digital Euro Association (DEA)

This project is led by the Digital Euro Association (DEA). The MiCAR Tracker is a DEA initiative to improve transparency in the digital-money ecosystem.

DEA mission & core pillars

The Digital Euro Association is an independent voice of society committed to advancing values such as freedom, privacy, and transparent governance in digital money.

What we do

We target new and innovative topics such as CBDCs and stablecoins, including emerging and novel forms of digital money and payment infrastructures.

Who we are

The Digital Euro Association (DEA) is a think tank specializing in central bank digital currencies (CBDCs), stablecoins and other forms of digital fiat currencies. In particular, we focus on the private and the public digital euro.

Mission

The Digital Euro Association (DEA) is committed to independence in aggregating and amplifying the views of society and the furtherance of the public good through knowledge exchange, encouraging new ideas, and forward-thinking in the field of digital money. Partnerships and collaborations are in no way an endorsement of member ideologies, products and services, nor political regimes.

DEA’s Core Pillars

  • Education: Publications, conferences, courses, working groups and more.
  • Community & Collaboration: Building multidisciplinary communities and cross-institutional knowledge exchange.
  • Public Affairs: Engaging with European bodies, advising on CBDC/stablecoin policy.

Contact & transparency

If you have corrections, suggestions, or questions about the MiCAR Tracker, please open an issue in the repository or contact the Digital Euro Association. We strive to be transparent about sources and methodology — if you’d like further detail on any data points, we’ll provide the sources behind them.
